# SuperSXO — Claim Policy

## Governing Principle

All content must be institutional, precise, strategic, and trustworthy.

Claims must be classified before publication. Unclassified claims may not be published.

---

## Claim Classification System

| Claim Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Doctrine claim** | A project-governing statement derived from the asset thesis |
| **General industry claim** | A broadly accepted industry statement that may still require caution |
| **Source-backed claim** | A factual or external claim that must be supported by a cited source |
| **Interpretation** | A strategic reading or analytical position held by this project |
| **Commercial claim** | A claim related to offers, products, reports, or monetization |
| **Blocked claim** | A prohibited or unsupported claim that may not be published |

---

## Approved Claim Examples

### Doctrine Claim (Approved)

> SEO brings the visitor. SXO governs what happens next.

> SuperSXO measures whether visibility becomes trust, clarity, and action.

Doctrine claims derive from the asset thesis and do not require external citation.

### Source-Backed Claim (Requires Citation)

Any claim referencing:

- specific ranking factors
- specific conversion statistics
- specific user behavior data
- external research or studies

must include a verified source reference before publication.

---

## Blocked Claim Examples

| Blocked Claim | Reason |
|---|---|
| "SuperSXO guarantees higher rankings or conversion growth." | Unsupported performance promise |
| "SuperSXO is the #1 SXO platform." | Unverifiable superlative |
| "Our method increases conversions by X%." | Unsupported statistical claim |
| Competitor weakness claims without evidence | Unverifiable and reputational risk |
| Fake urgency statements | Prohibited commercial practice |

---

## Content Prohibition List

All content must avoid:

- generic blog tone
- shallow SEO advice
- hype and exaggeration
- unsupported claims
- fake urgency
- exaggerated performance promises
- low-quality affiliate language
- filler content
- placeholder paragraphs
- recycled definitions
- generic "top 10 tips" content

---

## Claim Review Process

1. Identify the claim type before writing
2. If source-backed: locate and document the source before publishing
3. If commercial: confirm against `MONETIZATION_BOUNDARY.md`
4. If doctrine: verify alignment with the governing statement in `SYSTEM_ARCHITECTURE.md`
5. If unclear: classify as blocked until reviewed

Blocked claims must be documented in `DECISION_LOG.md` before removal or reclassification.

---

## Content Standard Summary

The project must prioritize:

- reference-grade content over volume
- precision over coverage
- institutional voice over conversational shortcuts
- defensible positioning over speculative authority
