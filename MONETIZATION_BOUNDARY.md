# SuperSXO — Monetization Boundary

## Governing Principle

The asset may generate income before sale, but **monetization must not damage strategic value**.

The income model must reinforce trust, not extract attention.

---

## Approved Monetization Directions

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| Free SuperSXO self-assessment | Trust-building tool that demonstrates asset value |
| Paid diagnostic reports | High-value, low-volume income with authority alignment |
| SXO audit reports | Commercial-grade deliverables tied to the SXO framework |
| Template packs | Scalable product with professional positioning |
| Agency licensing | Framework licensing for professional service providers |
| Selective tool partnerships | Only after authority is established |
| Strategic sponsorships | Only after authority is established, and only with aligned brands |

---

## Prohibited Monetization

The following monetization forms are prohibited:

- random display ads
- aggressive popups
- thin affiliate pages
- fake scarcity tactics
- low-trust lead traps
- irrelevant sponsorships
- monetization that makes the asset look cheap or generic

---

## Monetization Phasing

### Phase 1 — Foundation (Current)

No monetization elements.

Trust and authority are built before income is introduced.

### Phase 2 — First Offer

The first offer must be:

- directly tied to the SXO framework
- high-trust in format and language
- designed to reinforce authority, not dilute it

Approval required in `DECISION_LOG.md` before deployment.

### Phase 3 — Expanded Offers

Additional offers may be added only after:

- Phase 2 offer has demonstrated alignment with asset positioning
- Authority metrics show a positive trajectory
- No degradation of content quality is detected

Each new offer requires a log entry in `DECISION_LOG.md`.

### Phase 4 — Partnership and Licensing

Strategic partnerships and agency licensing require:

- documented partner alignment review
- confirmation that the partner brand does not damage asset dignity
- explicit approval logged in `DECISION_LOG.md`

---

## Buyer Logic Constraint

All monetization must remain compatible with the strategic acquisition story.

A buyer acquiring this asset must assess the income model as:

- credible
- scalable
- non-damaging to category authority

Any monetization that would cause a strategic buyer to reconsider acquisition is prohibited.

---

## Monetization Change Protocol

Any new monetization element requires:

1. Classification against the approved/prohibited lists above
2. A phasing review against the current phase
3. A log entry in `DECISION_LOG.md`
4. No deployment until all three steps are complete
