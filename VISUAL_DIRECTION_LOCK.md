# VISUAL DIRECTION LOCK — Search Experience Tribunal

**Status:** Locked  
**Applies to:** SuperSXO.com — all production interfaces  
**No implementation may proceed without satisfying the Implementation Gate defined below.**

---

## Core Concept

SuperSXO is not a website explaining SXO.

SuperSXO is a **sovereign Search Experience Tribunal**.

It examines whether search visibility deserves to become trust, clarity, navigation confidence, action, and strategic outcome. Every surface, every divider, every label must express that this examination is live, governed, and ongoing.

The visitor must feel as if they have entered an active adjudication system — not arrived at a landing page. The search-to-action journey is being examined as a case. The visitor is not browsing. They are inside proceedings.

---

## Visual Language

### Forbidden

- Law-firm imagery
- Gavels, scales, judges, courtroom clichés
- Generic SaaS layout or hero-button-sections rhythm
- Blog layout or editorial grid
- Dark dashboard or terminal interface
- Space, cosmic, or cyberpunk aesthetics
- Ordinary cards and lists
- Generic icon rows

### Required

- **White / off-white** as the sovereign default surface
- **Paper, record, and case-file surfaces** — tactile, institutional, undecorated
- **Layered adjudication chambers** — sections that read as procedural spaces, not content rows
- **Evidence sheets** — contained surfaces presenting material under examination
- **Procedural dividers** — rule lines that carry structural meaning, not decoration
- **Finding panels** — surfaces presenting examined conclusions
- **Verdict / outcome registers** — terminal surfaces where judgement is recorded
- **Controlled institutional blue** — authority, jurisdiction, doctrine
- **Trust green** — confirmation, established clarity
- **Muted amber / gold** — under examination, evidence, caution
- **Muted red** — reserved strictly for breakdown, failure, disqualification
- **Dark charcoal typography** — legible, formal, without softness
- **Subtle depth through layering** — surfaces stack and inset, they do not float

## Homepage — Opening Chamber / Case Intake

The homepage is not a landing page. It is the **Opening Chamber**.

When a visitor arrives, a case file is opening. The examination is already in progress.

### First Viewport — Required Concept

The first viewport must not resemble a conventional hero section. It must read as the opening surface of a live case file.

Required elements:

| Element | Value |
|---|---|
| Authority mark | SuperSXO |
| Case status | Active Examination |
| Docket | Search-to-Action |
| Jurisdiction | Search Experience Optimization |
| Primary question | Does visibility become trust, clarity, navigation confidence, action, and strategic outcome? |

The primary question is the governing statement of the entire interface. It sits at the centre of the opening chamber as the case surface — not inside a card, not under a headline, not beside a button. It is the case itself.

### Seven Articles Under Examination

The seven SXO layers must not appear as a list, a feature grid, an icon row, or a numbered section. They must appear as **articles under active examination** — procedural surfaces that are part of the case, not an index of services.

```
Article I    — Intent
Article II   — Visibility
Article III  — Experience
Article IV   — Trust
Article V    — Navigation
Article VI   — Action
Article VII  — Outcome
```

Each article is a governed layer of the examination. Each must read structurally as an evidentiary surface — with status, scope, and procedural weight — not as a feature description.

### Governed Action Routes

Three routes govern movement from the Opening Chamber:

1. **Enter the Framework** — leads to SXO Framework
2. **Begin Preliminary Assessment** — leads to SXO Score
3. **Request Formal Review** — leads to SXO Audit

These are not buttons. They are governed entry points. Their visual treatment must reflect institutional action routes — not calls to action on a marketing page.

## Inner Page Definitions

| Route | Tribunal Name | Procedural Role |
|---|---|---|
| `/what-is-sxo/` | Doctrine Record | Jurisdiction Statement — establishes the governing definition |
| `/sxo-framework/` | Examination Framework | The procedural structure under which all cases are examined |
| `/methodology/` | Procedural Rules | The rules of examination — how evidence is gathered and weighed |
| `/sxo-score/` | Preliminary Assessment | First-stage examination — preliminary findings on a route's readiness |
| `/sxo-audit/` | Formal Review | Full evidentiary examination — complete case review with governed findings |

Each inner page must express its tribunal role through structure, not through label alone. A Doctrine Record looks different from a Procedural Rules surface. The spatial treatment, the dividers, the surface depth, and the typographic register must all reinforce the procedural identity of the page.

## Rejected Current Direction

The current implementation is formally rejected as the production visual direction.

**Rejection is not based on execution quality. It is based on conceptual failure.**

The current interface:

- Uses a conventional webpage rhythm — hero, sections, footer — which contradicts the tribunal concept at the structural level
- Relies on judicial labels applied to standard layout components — the labels say "chamber" but the structure says "webpage"
- Does not immerse the visitor — the visitor remains outside the system, reading about it, rather than inside it, experiencing it
- Does not translate the conceptual story at a structural depth — the concept is described, not expressed
- Does not feel reference-grade — it does not achieve the institutional authority or spatial coherence that the Search Experience Tribunal concept requires

Applying light colours and institutional vocabulary to a conventional layout is not an implementation of the tribunal concept. It is a labelled approximation of it. That approximation is rejected.

---

## Implementation Gate

No production redesign may begin until all five conditions are satisfied and approved:

1. **A mobile-first visual mockup is produced and approved.** The mockup is the governing artefact. No implementation precedes it.

2. **The mockup clearly demonstrates the Search Experience Tribunal concept** — not through labels, but through the spatial organisation, surface treatment, typographic register, and procedural logic of the interface itself.

3. **The first viewport no longer resembles a conventional landing page** in any respect. The opening chamber must be immediately legible as an adjudication surface, not a hero section with judicial vocabulary.

4. **The seven SXO layers are visually integrated as articles under examination** — with procedural structure, evidentiary weight, and formal status — not presented as a list, grid, or feature index.

5. **The interface reads as white, institutional, immersive, and sovereign.** A visitor encountering it for the first time must feel they have entered a governed system, not arrived at a website.
